What it actually does

This isn't a willpower problem.
It's what the habit does to your brain.

None of this is about shame or morality. It's the same reward machinery that other addictions hijack, and the changes show up on brain scans. Knowing the mechanism is the first thing that makes it beatable.

The wiring changes

Porn pushes dopamine harder than ordinary life ever can. In heavy users, scans show less grey matter in the parts of the brain that handle self-control — the same footprint researchers find in drug dependence.

Real life goes flat

To cope with the flood, the brain dials its sensitivity down. You end up needing more, and harder, just to feel the same hit. Meanwhile work, hobbies and real closeness start to feel dull by comparison.

It costs you elsewhere

Studies tie heavy use to anxiety, low mood and flagging motivation, to erection problems in young men, and to less satisfying relationships. The brain's bonding chemistry gets pointed at a screen instead of a person.

The part most people get wrong

You're not weak.
Your brain has been trained.

Willpower runs out — that's just how it works, and the numbers bear it out: most people who genuinely want to stop can't do it on grit alone. So NSFW Guard takes the decision off your plate. By the time your eyes register what's there, the screen is already covered. The question stops being "can I resist this?" and becomes "what do I want to do with today's clean streak?"

What's inside

Built for people who are serious about stopping.

Watches in real time

An on-device model checks your screen a couple of times a second and flags porn, hentai and explicit imagery wherever it appears — any app, any site.

Every monitor, no gaps

Each display gets its own watcher and its own cover. Plug in a second screen and coverage extends to it automatically.

Catches the small stuff

Besides the whole screen, it scores a grid of smaller regions each pass, so a thumbnail in the corner doesn't slip through.

A partner holds the key

You can't switch it off by yourself. Someone you trust sets the password during setup, and a built-in cooldown blocks spur-of-the-moment bypasses.

Hard to kill

A small guardian keeps an eye on the app. Close it through Task Manager and it comes back on its own within seconds.

Streaks you can see

Your clean streak ticks up live. Every block is logged with a time and reason, and you can export the lot as a CSV for check-ins.

A way through the urge

One tap opens a slow guided-breathing screen for the hard moments — something to do with your hands while the wave passes.

Offline, for good

The model ships with the app, so there's no internet needed after install and nothing to log in to. Free, with no paid tier waiting behind a wall.
